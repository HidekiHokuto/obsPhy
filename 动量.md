In [[牛顿力学]], momentum is the product of the mass and velocity of an object. It is a vector quantity, possessing a magnitude and a direction. If $m$ is an object's mass and $v$ is its velocity (also a vector quantity), then the object's momentum $p$ is: $\vb*{p} = m \vb*{v}$.
